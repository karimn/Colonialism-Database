Welcome to the CLIO World Tables

The CLIO World Tables include historical data pertaining to a wide range of demographic, economic, political, and socio-cultural phenomena. The database draws on diverse set of sources – published and unpublished, hard copy and digital, archival and non-archival, English and foreign language. Longitudinal coverage extends back at least a century, and in a few instances for millennia. Spatial coverage is global, which is to say that every variable included in the collection spans at least two continents.

This data speaks to a wide range of problems and questions including colonialism/imperialism, conflict (intra- and inter-state), democracy, development, the diffusion of cultures, globalization and trade, nation and state formation, religion, revolution, and slavery – to name only a few. Indeed, one can scarcely predict the uses which this varied collection of evidence might serve – for students, teachers, academics, or policymakers. CLIO is intended to be a public good, providing free access to historical data about diverse topics. We ask three things of our users:

  • Register on the site. This helps us maintain site security, gives us a bit of information about our end-users (which we can report to our funders), and helps us chart the future direction of CLIO. It also allows us to inform you when there are important additions or renovations to the site (if you indicate a wish to be contacted). All contact information is strictly confidential and will not be shared with anyone or used for commercial purposes.
  • Cite any data drawn from the site (a) according to the original data source(s) indicated in the Source field and (b) its immediate source, CLIO. Thus, if you are using data on land area whose original source is Union Postale Universelle Statistique Generale, you should cite this source with the additional notation “drawn from CLIO World Tables”, along with our web URL or our project document (see below).
  • Help us improve CLIO by (a) making us aware of problems with the data or data descriptions, (b) contributing your own data to the site, and (c) making suggestions about data sources that we might not be aware of. These will be integrated periodically to the database.
